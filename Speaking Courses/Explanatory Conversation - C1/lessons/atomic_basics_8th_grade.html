üè† Electron Shells and Chemical Bonding

The Bonding Rule

Atoms want their outermost shell to be full

They'll share, give, or take electrons to achieve this

üî¢ Shell Capacity

  • 1st shell: Holds 2 electrons maximum
  • 2nd shell: Holds 8 electrons maximum
  • 3rd shell: Holds 8 electrons (simplified)
  • Outer shell: Determines bonding behavior

üéØ What Atoms Want

  • Full outer shell: Stable, happy atoms
  • Almost full: Want to gain electrons
  • Almost empty: Want to lose electrons
  • Half full: Want to share electrons

ü§ù How They Bond

  • Give/Take: Ionic bonding (opposites attract)
  • Share: Covalent bonding (sharing is caring)
  • Result: Both atoms get full outer shells
  • Outcome: New compound with new properties

üåä How Water Forms

  • Oxygen: Has 6 outer electrons, wants 2 more
  • Hydrogen: Has 1 electron, wants 1 more
  • Solution: Share electrons between atoms
  • Result: O gets 8, each H gets 2 (full shells!)

‚ú® Why Sharing Works

  • Shared electrons: Count for both atoms
  • No charges: Atoms stay neutral
  • Strong bonds: Atoms hold tightly together
  • New properties: Water is liquid, not gas

Remember the Difference

Ionic: "I'll give you my electron" (metals + non-metals)

Covalent: "Let's share electrons" (non-metals + non-metals)

🔬 What is an Atom?

+

🔴 Nucleus (Center)

  • Contains: Protons (+) and Neutrons (neutral)
  • Size: Extremely tiny but very heavy
  • Job: Determines what element it is

🔵 Electrons (Outside)

  • Charge: Negative (-)
  • Location: Moving around nucleus in "shells"
  • Job: Determine how atoms bond together

Think of it Like...

Solar System: Nucleus = Sun, Electrons = Planets orbiting

School: Nucleus = Main Office, Electrons = Students in different grades

🧩 The Three Main Particles

⚡ PROTONS

  • Charge: Positive (+)
  • Location: In the nucleus
  • Key Fact: Number of protons = Element identity
  • Remember: Like an ID number for elements

⚪ NEUTRONS

  • Charge: Neutral (0)
  • Location: In the nucleus with protons
  • Key Fact: Add mass and stability
  • Remember: Like glue holding nucleus together

⚡ ELECTRONS

  • Charge: Negative (-)
  • Location: Moving around nucleus
  • Key Fact: Determine chemical behavior
  • Remember: Like hands that grab onto other atoms
Key Rule: Normal atoms have equal numbers of protons and electrons (neutral overall)

🏷️ What Makes Each Element Unique

The Big Idea

The number of protons determines the element

Change protons = completely different element

1️⃣ Hydrogen

  • Protons: 1
  • Properties: Lightest gas
  • Found in: Water (H₂O)

6️⃣ Carbon

  • Protons: 6
  • Properties: Forms many bonds
  • Found in: All living things

🅾️ Oxygen

  • Protons: 8
  • Properties: Very reactive
  • Found in: Air we breathe

📝 How It's Organized

  • Order: By number of protons (atomic number)
  • Rows: Same number of electron shells
  • Columns: Similar chemical behavior
  • Pattern: Properties repeat in patterns

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Element Families

  • Noble gases: Don't react (full outer shell)
  • Alkali metals: Very reactive metals
  • Halogens: Very reactive non-metals
  • Pattern: Same column = similar behavior

🏠 Electron Shells and Chemical Bonding

The Bonding Rule

Atoms want their outermost shell to be full

They'll share, give, or take electrons to achieve this

🔢 Shell Capacity

  • 1st shell: Holds 2 electrons maximum
  • 2nd shell: Holds 8 electrons maximum
  • 3rd shell: Holds 8 electrons (simplified)
  • Outer shell: Determines bonding behavior

🎯 What Atoms Want

  • Full outer shell: Stable, happy atoms
  • Almost full: Want to gain electrons
  • Almost empty: Want to lose electrons
  • Half full: Want to share electrons

🤝 How They Bond

  • Give/Take: Ionic bonding (opposites attract)
  • Share: Covalent bonding (sharing is caring)
  • Result: Both atoms get full outer shells
  • Outcome: New compound with new properties

🟡 Sodium's Story

  • Has: 11 electrons (2, 8, 1)
  • Wants: To lose 1 electron from outer shell
  • Becomes: Na⁺ ion (positive charge)
  • Result: Full outer shell with 8 electrons

🟢 Chlorine's Story

  • Has: 17 electrons (2, 8, 7)
  • Wants: To gain 1 electron for outer shell
  • Becomes: Cl⁻ ion (negative charge)
  • Result: Full outer shell with 8 electrons
Opposites Attract: Na⁺ and Cl⁻ stick together to form salt crystals!

🌊 How Water Forms

  • Oxygen: Has 6 outer electrons, wants 2 more
  • Hydrogen: Has 1 electron, wants 1 more
  • Solution: Share electrons between atoms
  • Result: O gets 8, each H gets 2 (full shells!)

✨ Why Sharing Works

  • Shared electrons: Count for both atoms
  • No charges: Atoms stay neutral
  • Strong bonds: Atoms hold tightly together
  • New properties: Water is liquid, not gas

Remember the Difference

Ionic: "I'll give you my electron" (metals + non-metals)

Covalent: "Let's share electrons" (non-metals + non-metals)

🎯 Putting It All Together

The Big Picture

Atoms combine in predictable ways to form all materials around us

🔑 Key Concepts

  • Atomic structure: Protons determine element identity
  • Periodic table: Organized by atomic number
  • Electron shells: Outer shell determines bonding
  • Chemical bonds: Ionic (give/take) or covalent (share)

🌟 Real Examples

  • Salt (NaCl): Ionic bonding creates table salt
  • Water (H₂O): Covalent bonding creates liquid water
  • Carbon dioxide (CO₂): Covalent bonds in gas we exhale
  • Calcium chloride: Ionic compound used to melt ice

🔬 Why This Matters

  • Predicting properties: Structure determines behavior
  • Making new materials: Engineers design compounds
  • Understanding reactions: Why chemicals react
  • Medicine: How drugs work in our bodies

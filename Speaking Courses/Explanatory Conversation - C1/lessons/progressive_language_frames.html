üîÑ Cause and Effect Frames

Frame Set 1: Explaining Why Things Happened
"_____ caused _____ because _____."
Example: "Industrialization caused urban overcrowding because factory jobs attracted workers to cities."
"As a result of _____, _____."
Example: "As a result of unsafe factory conditions, workers demanded government regulation."
"_____ led to _____, which then caused _____."
Example: "Muckraker investigations led to public outrage, which then caused Congress to pass reform laws."
"The main reason _____ happened was _____."
Example: "The main reason Progressive reforms succeeded was strong public pressure for change."

‚öñÔ∏è Before/After Comparison Frames

Frame Set 2: Showing Historical Change
"Before _____, _____. After _____, _____."
Example: "Before Progressive reforms, government rarely regulated business. After the reforms, federal agencies monitored working conditions."
"In the 1890s, _____. By 1920, _____."
Example: "In the 1890s, women could not vote. By 1920, the 19th Amendment gave women voting rights."
"_____ transformed _____ from _____ to _____."
Example: "Progressive reforms transformed American government from laissez-faire to regulatory state."

üìÑ Evidence Analysis Frames

Frame Set 3: Using Historical Evidence
"Evidence of _____ can be seen in _____."
Example: "Evidence of poor working conditions can be seen in Upton Sinclair's description of meatpacking plants."
"_____ shows that _____ because _____."
Example: "The Pure Food and Drug Act shows that muckrakers were effective because it was passed right after 'The Jungle' was published."
"According to _____, _____."
Example: "According to Jacob Riis's photographs, tenement conditions were overcrowded and unsanitary."
"This demonstrates _____ because _____."
Example: "This demonstrates Progressive faith in government solutions because they created so many new federal agencies."

‚≠ê Evaluation and Assessment Frames

Frame Set 4: Making Historical Judgments
"_____ was successful because _____."
Example: "The women's suffrage movement was successful because it achieved its main goal of voting rights."
"_____ had limited success because _____."
Example: "Progressive labor reforms had limited success because workers still faced opposition from business owners."
"The most important _____ was _____ because _____."
Example: "The most important Progressive reform was government regulation of business because it protected consumers and workers."
"Overall, _____ can be considered _____ because _____."
Example: "Overall, the Progressive Era can be considered partially successful because it solved some problems but left others unaddressed."

üìÖ Chronological Sequence Frames

Frame Set 5: Organizing Historical Events
"First, _____. Then, _____. Finally, _____."
Example: "First, muckrakers exposed problems. Then, public pressure built. Finally, Congress passed reform laws."
"During the _____, _____."
Example: "During the Progressive Era, Americans focused on solving social problems through government action."
"By _____, _____ had _____."
Example: "By 1920, the federal government had created many new regulatory agencies."
"The period from _____ to _____ was characterized by _____."
Example: "The period from 1890 to 1920 was characterized by efforts to reform industrial society."

üîó Connection and Significance Frames

Frame Set 6: Linking Events and Ideas
"_____ is connected to _____ because _____."
Example: "Industrialization is connected to Progressive reform because industrial problems created the need for government solutions."
"_____ is significant because _____."
Example: "The Progressive Era is significant because it established the principle that government should solve social problems."
"The legacy of _____ can be seen in _____."
Example: "The legacy of Progressive reforms can be seen in modern government agencies like the FDA."
"_____ continues to influence _____ today because _____."
Example: "Progressive Era reforms continue to influence American politics today because we still debate the role of government in solving problems."

Frame Set 7: Understanding Different Viewpoints
"_____ believed _____ because _____."
Example: "Progressives believed government should regulate business because they saw how unregulated industry harmed workers."
"From the perspective of _____, _____."
Example: "From the perspective of factory owners, regulations were unnecessary government interference."
"While _____ supported _____, _____ opposed it because _____."
Example: "While workers supported safety regulations, business owners opposed it because it increased their costs."

Building Complete Historical Arguments

Topic: Why Progressive Reforms Had Limited Success

"Progressive reforms had limited success."
"Evidence of this can be seen in racial exclusion."
"Most reforms helped white Americans but not African Americans."
"This demonstrates that Progressives focused on some problems while ignoring others."
"Overall, the era can be considered partially successful."
Full Argument: "Progressive reforms had limited success because they only addressed certain problems. Evidence of this can be seen in racial exclusion - most reforms helped white Americans but not African Americans. This demonstrates that Progressives focused on some problems while ignoring others. Overall, the era can be considered partially successful because it solved some industrial problems but failed to address racial inequality."

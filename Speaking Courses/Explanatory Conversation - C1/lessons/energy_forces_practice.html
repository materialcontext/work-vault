‚ö° Language for Describing Energy

Types and Properties of Energy
"Energy is the ability to do work or cause change, and it comes in many different forms."
"Kinetic energy is the energy of motion, while potential energy is stored energy due to position."
"Chemical energy is stored in the bonds between atoms and can be found in food, fuel, and batteries."
Energy Transfer and Transformation
"Energy can transfer from one object to another, like when hot coffee warms your hands."
"Energy can also transform from one type to another, such as when chemical energy becomes kinetic energy."
"During this process, the total amount of energy stays the same because energy cannot be created or destroyed."

üí™ Language for Describing Forces

What Forces Are and Do
"A force is a push or pull that can change how an object moves."
"Forces can start motion, stop motion, or change direction, and they can also change the shape of objects."
"Every force has both strength and direction, and forces always come in pairs."
Observable Effects of Forces
"When you apply a force to an object at rest, it begins to move."
"If you increase the force, the object will accelerate faster."
"Friction is a force that opposes motion and converts kinetic energy into heat energy."

üîó Cause-and-Effect Language for Energy and Forces

Energy Causes Changes
"When chemical energy in your muscles is released, it creates forces that move your body."
"Because the ball has kinetic energy, it can transfer that energy to other objects when it collides."
"As a result of gravitational force, potential energy transforms into kinetic energy when objects fall."
Forces Create Energy Changes
"When friction acts on a moving object, it converts kinetic energy into thermal energy."
"The force from your muscles transfers chemical energy to kinetic energy in the objects you move."
"This happens because forces are the mechanism by which energy moves and transforms."

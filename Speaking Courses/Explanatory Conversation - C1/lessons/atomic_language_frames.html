üìù Basic Description Frames

Frame Set 1: Describing Structure
"An atom consists of _____ and _____."
Example: "An atom consists of a nucleus and electrons."
"The _____ contains _____ and _____."
Example: "The nucleus contains protons and neutrons."
"_____ are located _____ the nucleus."
Example: "Electrons are located around the nucleus."
Practice Task: Use each frame to describe a different part of atomic structure

‚ö° Property Description Frames

Frame Set 2: Describing Characteristics
"_____ have a _____ charge."
Example: "Protons have a positive charge."
"The _____ is extremely _____ but very _____."
Example: "The nucleus is extremely small but very heavy."
"_____ move around the nucleus in _____."
Example: "Electrons move around the nucleus in shells."
Your turn: Fill in the blanks
"_____ are _____ particles that determine _____."
(Hint: Talk about neutrons)

üîß Function Explanation Frames

Frame Set 3: Explaining What Things Do
"The number of _____ determines _____."
Example: "The number of protons determines what element it is."
"_____ are responsible for _____."
Example: "Electrons are responsible for chemical bonding."
"_____ provide _____ to the atom."
Example: "Neutrons provide mass and stability to the atom."
"The _____ controls _____ because _____."
Example: "The outer shell controls bonding behavior because atoms want full shells."

‚öñÔ∏è Comparison Frames

Frame Set 4: Comparing and Contrasting
"Unlike _____, _____ have _____."
Example: "Unlike protons, neutrons have no charge."
"Both _____ and _____ are _____, but _____."
Example: "Both protons and neutrons are in the nucleus, but only protons have charge."
"While _____ are _____, _____ are _____."
Example: "While protons are positive, electrons are negative."
Your turn: Compare ionic and covalent bonding
"_____ bonding involves _____, while _____ bonding involves _____."

üîÑ Cause and Effect Frames

Frame Set 5: Explaining Why Things Happen
"Because _____, _____."
Example: "Because atoms want full outer shells, they form chemical bonds."
"When _____, _____ happens."
Example: "When sodium loses an electron, it becomes positively charged."
"_____ results in _____ because _____."
Example: "Sharing electrons results in covalent bonds because both atoms get full shells."
"The reason _____ is that _____."
Example: "The reason noble gases don't react is that they already have full outer shells."

üîó Analogy Frames

Frame Set 6: Making Comparisons to Familiar Things
"Think of _____ like _____."
Example: "Think of an atom like a solar system."
"_____ is similar to _____ because both _____."
Example: "The nucleus is similar to the sun because both are at the center."
"Just like _____, _____ also _____."
Example: "Just like planets orbit the sun, electrons also move around the nucleus."
"You can imagine _____ as _____ that _____."
Example: "You can imagine chemical bonding as friendship that makes both atoms happy."

Frame Set 7: Explaining Steps and Processes

"The process begins when _____."
Example: "The process begins when atoms with incomplete outer shells meet."
"After _____, the next step is _____."
Example: "After electrons are shared, the next step is forming a stable molecule."

üèóÔ∏è Assembly Practice: Level 2

Combining 4-5 Frames with Transitions

Topic: How Ionic Bonding Works

"Atoms want full outer shells."
"Sodium has 1 outer electron."
"Because of this, sodium loses an electron."
"Chlorine needs 1 electron."
"Therefore, they form an ionic bond."
Full Explanation: "Atoms want full outer shells. Sodium has 1 outer electron, so because of this, sodium loses an electron. Chlorine needs 1 electron to complete its shell. Therefore, when they meet, they form an ionic bond that makes both atoms stable."

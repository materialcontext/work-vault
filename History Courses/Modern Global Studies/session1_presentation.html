üîç The ESCAPE Method

Professional Historical Analysis Framework

Historians use systematic methods to analyze primary sources. Today you'll learn the ESCAPE method - a six-step process for understanding historical documents.

E - Evidence

What specific information does the source provide? What doesn't it provide?

S - Source

Who created this source and what type of source is it? How might this shape the point of view?

C - Context

What were the historical circumstances surrounding the source's creation that might influence its content?

A - Audience

Who was this written for and how might that shape the tone and conent? Was it public or private

P - Purpose

Why was this written? What did the author want to accomplish?

E - Evaluate

How reliable and useful is this source for understanding the historical period or event in question?

üéØ Let's Practice ESCAPE

"All students must wear school ID badges while on campus. This policy begins Monday, October 15th. Questions should be directed to the main office."

‚Äî School Principal, October 10th announcement

Quick ESCAPE Analysis

  • E - Evidence: Specific start date, clear directive
  • S - Source: School announcement from principal
  • C - Context: New security policy, mid-October timing
  • A - Audience: Students, parents, staff
  • P - Purpose: Inform about new rule, ensure compliance
  • E - Evaluate: Missing - why this policy? What consequences?

üîç Bias vs. Perspective Analysis

Understanding the Difference

All historical sources have perspective - that's normal and expected. Bias becomes problematic when it involves unfair prejudice or deliberate distortion.

‚úÖ Normal Perspective

Columbus writes to his sponsors, so he emphasizes potential profits and downplays risks - this makes sense given his audience and purpose

‚ö†Ô∏è Problematic Bias

Columbus assumes Indigenous people have "no religion" because it's different from Christianity - this shows cultural prejudice

‚úÖ Strategic Perspective

Las Casas emphasizes worst abuses to reform Spanish policy - his goal explains his emphasis

‚ö†Ô∏è Potential Bias

Las Casas might exaggerate numbers to strengthen his argument - we'd need other sources to verify

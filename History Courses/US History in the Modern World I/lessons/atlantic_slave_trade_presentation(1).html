üìú Royal African Company Charter (1672)

Royal African Company Charter (1672):
"We do give and grant unto the said Company... the whole, entire and only trade and traffic... for the buying and selling bartering and exchanging of, for, or with any slaves, negroes, and other persons whatsoever... and of all manner of goods, wares, and merchandises whatsoever to be vended or found in any the ports, havens, creeks, or places of Africa..."

Document Analysis Framework:

üèõÔ∏è Legal Innovation

Corporate organization: Joint-stock company structure
Geographic scope: "Whole and entire" African trade

üí∞ Economic Structure

Monopoly profits: Exclusive rights eliminate competition

Government partnership: Crown authority supports commercial profits

üåç Revolutionary Scope

Continental control: Claims authority over entire African coast
Human commodification: People treated as "goods and merchandises"
Systematic organization: Corporate structure for slave trade

‚öñÔ∏è Spanish vs. British Slave Systems

Revolutionary Differences in Colonial Labor Organization

üá™üá∏ Spanish Colonial System

  • Legal framework: Siete Partidas laws recognize slave rights to marriage, property, self-purchase
  • Religious integration: Catholic Church requires slave baptism and religious instruction
  • Social mobility: Manumission practices create free black populations
  • Economic focus: Mining and urban crafts alongside agricultural labor
  • Racial hierarchy: Complex caste system with intermediate categories

üá¨üáß British Colonial System

  • Legal framework: Slave codes define enslaved people as property with minimal rights
  • Religious exclusion: Protestant churches generally exclude enslaved people from membership
  • Social rigidity: Limited manumission creates stark racial boundaries
  • Economic focus: Plantation agriculture specialization for export markets
  • Racial hierarchy: Binary system: white freedom vs. black slavery
How do these different approaches to slavery create different revolutionary possibilities for colonial social development?

üîÑ The Spoils System

"To the victor belong the spoils"

Jackson's Logic: Democracy means rotation in office, not permanent bureaucracy. Ordinary people deserve government jobs.

Reality: Fires federal employees, replaces with political supporters. Party loyalty becomes job requirement.

The Jacksonian Paradox

Democratic and Undemocratic Simultaneously

‚öñÔ∏è Jackson's Complex Legacy

‚úÖ Jackson WAS Democratic

  • Expanded Voting Rights: Supported removing property requirements for white men
  • Common Man Appeal: First president from frontier, not aristocratic elite
  • Popular Campaigning: Used mass rallies, democratic participation
  • Anti-Elite Rhetoric: Attacked "corrupt bargains" and wealthy privilege
  • Spoils System: Gave government jobs to ordinary supporters
  • Majority Rule: Claimed to represent will of the people

‚ùå Jackson Was UNDEMOCRATIC

  • Indian Removal: Ignored Supreme Court, used force against Cherokee Nation
  • Slavery Support: Defended and expanded slavery westward
  • Racial Exclusion: Democracy only for white men, others excluded
  • Presidential Power: Concentrated executive authority, "King Andrew"
  • Ignored Courts: Defied Supreme Court in Worcester v. Georgia
  • Violence and Force: Used military power against political opponents

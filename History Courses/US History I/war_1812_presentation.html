Our Primary Source

Dolley Madison's Letter

To her sister Anna Cutts

August 24, 1814

What makes this special?

  • Written as events were happening
  • Personal letter to family (private thoughts)
  • First Lady's perspective during crisis
  • Shows both fear and courage

Your observations...

"Since sunrise I have been turning my spy-glass in every direction... but, alas! I can descry only groups of military, wandering in all directions, as if there was a lack of arms, or of spirit to fight for their own fireside..."

Evidence of American Weakness?

  • "wandering in all directions"
  • "lack of arms"
  • "lack of spirit to fight"
  • Capital under attack

"We have had a battle, or skirmish, near Bladensburg, and here I am still, within sound of the cannon! Mr. Madison comes not. May God protect us! Two messengers come to bid me fly; but here I mean to wait for him..."

Evidence of American Strength?

  • Dolley's courage under fire
  • Loyalty to husband and country
  • Determined to save important documents
  • Refuses to abandon her post

Analyzing Our Source

SOAPS Method

  • Speaker: Dolley Madison (First Lady)
  • Occasion: British attack on Washington
  • Audience: Her sister Anna (personal letter)
  • Purpose: Inform family, record events, express fears
  • Subject: Chaos, fear, uncertainty, but also loyalty
